## Who Must File VAT Returns in Dubai?

Any VAT-registered business in Dubai must file returns, regardless of whether tax is payable for that period.

This includes:
- Mainland LLCs
- Free zone entities conducting taxable supplies
- E-commerce operators
- Consultancy firms
- Import/export businesses

Even if no VAT is due, submission remains mandatory. The FTA requires companies to maintain financial records for at least five years to ensure transparency and audit readiness.

## VAT Return Filing Frequency and Deadlines

VAT returns are typically filed:
- Quarterly (standard for most SMEs)
- Monthly (for larger entities assigned by the FTA)

The deadline is 28 days after the end of the tax period.

For example:
If your tax period ends on June 30, the VAT return must be submitted by July 28.

## Step-by-Step VAT Return Filing Process

Let’s break it down into structured stages.

### 1. Maintain Accurate Financial Records
Proper documentation forms the backbone of compliance. Required records include:
- Sales invoices
- Purchase invoices
- Credit and debit notes
- Import/export documentation
- Supplier Tax Registration Numbers (TRNs)

Without accurate record-keeping, VAT calculations become unreliable. Many Dubai companies conduct internal reconciliations at least two weeks before filing deadlines to allow room for corrections.

### 2. Calculate Output VAT
Output VAT refers to VAT collected from customers on taxable supplies. For example, if a consultancy firm invoices clients for taxable services, the VAT charged must be recorded as output VAT.

Accurate classification is essential, especially when distinguishing between:
- Standard-rated supplies
- Zero-rated supplies
- Exempt supplies

Misclassification is one of the most common filing errors in Dubai.

### 3. Calculate Input VAT
Input VAT is the VAT paid on eligible business expenses. Businesses may recover VAT on:
- Office rent (where applicable)
- Professional services
- Equipment purchases
- Operational expenses

However, certain expenses may not qualify for recovery under UAE VAT regulations. A logistics firm in Al Quoz once identified unrecoverable VAT claims incorrectly during a quarterly review. Corrective disclosure avoided further complications. Careful evaluation is critical.

### 4. Determine Net VAT Payable or Recoverable
VAT payable is calculated as:
**Output VAT – Input VAT**

If output VAT exceeds input VAT, the business must remit the difference. If input VAT exceeds output VAT, the excess may be carried forward or subject to refund procedures. Accurate reconciliation ensures businesses neither overpay nor underreport liabilities.

### 5. Submit VAT Return Through the FTA Portal
VAT returns are filed electronically via the FTA’s online portal. The return form requires reporting of the following:
- Total taxable supplies
- Zero-rated supplies
- Exempt supplies
- Standard-rated purchases
- Imports subject to VAT
- Adjustments (if applicable)

## FAQs

**Q1: How often must VAT returns be filed in Dubai?**
Most businesses file quarterly, while some larger entities are assigned monthly filing periods by the FTA.

**Q2: Is VAT return filing mandatory even if there are no transactions?**
Yes. VAT-registered businesses must submit returns even if there are no taxable activities during the period.

**Q3: What documents are required for VAT filing in Dubai?**
Businesses need sales invoices, purchase invoices, credit/debit notes, TRNs, and import/export documentation.

**Q4: Can businesses correct errors in previously submitted VAT returns?**
Yes. Adjustments or voluntary disclosures may be submitted according to FTA guidelines.
