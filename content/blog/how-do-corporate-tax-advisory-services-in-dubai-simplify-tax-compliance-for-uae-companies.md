## What Is Corporate Tax in the UAE?

Corporate tax is a federal tax applied to business profits. While the UAE has long been known for being a tax-free haven, the introduction of corporate tax marked an important shift, one that encourages transparency, international compliance, and sustainable economic growth.

For many businesses in Dubai, the first reaction was uncertainty. What counts as taxable income? How do free zones fit in? What about cross-border transactions? This is the point where Corporate Tax Advisory Services prove indispensable.

## FAQ

**1. What is the UAE corporate tax rate?**  
Most businesses are taxed at 9% on taxable profits above a specific threshold.

**2. Do free zone companies need to pay corporate tax?**  
Some qualify for 0% tax incentives, but only if they meet the UAE’s strict conditions.

**3. Is transfer pricing required in the UAE?**  
Yes. Companies with related-party transactions must maintain proper documentation.

**4. Who needs to register for corporate tax?**  
All taxable persons, including free zone companies and foreign businesses with UAE operations.

**5. Can corporate tax returns be filed late?**  
Late filings may lead to penalties; advisory support helps prevent this.
